Reverse mortgage mechanics. HECM (Home Equity Conversion Mortgage) is the federally-insured RM available at 62+. The Principal Limit Factor (PLF) determines borrowing capacity as a % of home value, scaling from ~41% at 62 to ~58% at 80. Upfront costs (~2% MIP + origination + closing, ~$15-20k typical) are deducted from initial availability. Two strategies modeled: LOC Backstop opens the line of credit when first needed, lets it grow at the loan accrual rate, and taps it only when the liquid portfolio runs out — typically the most efficient HECM use. Tenure takes a guaranteed lifetime monthly payment from a chosen start age, computed using HUD's age-100 horizon. Loan balance compounds at the HECM rate; the loan is non-recourse, so heirs owe the lesser of loan balance or 95% of home value. Net legacy = max(0, home_value − loan_balance) + other_physical + remaining portfolio.
Educational tool — not financial advice. PLF is approximated; actual values depend on the expected interest rate set by HUD at origination. Spousal eligibility (both 62+ as borrowers), property tax / insurance / maintenance obligations, and primary-residence requirements are not modeled. Loss of home equity reduces inheritance — coordinate with estate planning. Validate against a NRMLA-certified counselor and a fee-only fiduciary CFP.
